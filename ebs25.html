Event-based Sensing (EBS25)

Topic Leaders

  • Andre Van Schaik, Director International Centre for Neuromorphic Systems (ICNS), Western Sydney University (WSU), Australia
  • Piotr Dudek, University of Manchester, UK

Goals

This work group aims to advance the development and application of neuromorphic sensors, including dynamic vision sensors (DVS), SCAMP, neuromorphic radar, and olfactory sensors. By leveraging the inherent advantages of neuromorphic sensors such as low power consumption, low data rate, and high dynamic range, we seek to revolutionize multiple fields. Our goals include enhancing satellite imaging and situational awareness through neuromorphic cameras in space, advancing SCAMP cameras for high-speed, low-latency image processing in robotics and surveillance, and developing neuromorphic radar systems for superior object detection and environmental sensing. Additionally, we aim to innovate olfactory sensors for health diagnostics and environmental monitoring. Through fostering collaboration between academia and industry, providing hands-on experience, and organizing workshops and seminars, we intend to accelerate the commercialization of neuromorphic technologies and significantly contribute to the scientific community.

Broad Project Themes

  • Implementing neuromorphic algorithms for space navigation, visual attention, and control.
  • Developing high-speed, low-latency image processing algorithms for robotics and surveillance.
  • Integrating SCAMP cameras with other neuromorphic sensors for multi-modal sensing systems.
  • Designing and implementing neuromorphic radar sensors for superior object detection and environmental sensing.
  • Developing real-time signal processing and pattern recognition algorithms for radar applications.
  • Developing novel algorithms for neuromorphic odour recognition and classification.
  • Applying olfactory sensors to health diagnostics through breath analysis and environmental monitoring.
  • Developing advanced signal processing techniques using neuromorphic principles.
